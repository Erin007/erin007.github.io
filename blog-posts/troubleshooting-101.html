Troubleshooting Ruby 101

September 22, 2016

.

Programmers are well-aware of the health consequences of our screen-obsessed sedentary lifestyles - stiff necks, sore backs. When you're new, no one tells you about the permament bruise you'll have on your forehead from all of the facepalming.

.

Seriously. I say things like "Come on, Ruby, why do you have to be such a finnicky *****?!?". (Yes, I speak to Ruby. My penchant for anthropomorphism drives my biologist husband bonkers.) When you are first learning the syntax of a new language, every little thing breaks your code.

.

These are 10 actual facepalm moments I experienced while learning Ruby. I offer them so that, perhaps, you will not make the same mistakes.

    .

  1. 1. Periods have to go inside quotes. You can't have stray punctuation thrown in like confetti.
  2. 2. Initialize is spelled I-N-I-T-I-A-L-I-Z-E. Ruby won't understand if you spell it any other way - especially if you are really confused and type "initiate" instead.
  3. 3. When Ruby hits a return inside of a loop, that's it. The loop's done running.
  4. 4. Methods can't call themselves. Well, they can, but it's called recursion and you should only do it on purpose and with an attainable base case.
  5. 5. 'Single quotes' don't work for string interpolation nor do they get the job done for escape sequences.
  6. 6. Require and require relative are different. Do you want to require something like a gem? Or would you like to require relative a file in the same or a nearby directory?
  7. 7. Else won't take a conditional. Assume anything you haven't covered in an if or elsif conditional will automatically slide into this category.
  8. 8. If Ruby wants 2 arguements, you give her 2 arguements. There's no negotiating.
  9. 9. Keep your coverage in your .gitignore. Ain't nobody got time for that kind of scrolling.
  10. 10. = and == both have a role to play, and those roles are very different.
